AI Meeting Summarization for Medical Practices

By Dr. Lisa Montgomery, Chief of Surgery June 4, 2026 Medical Technology

I am a practicing surgeon. Every week, I attend two hours of tumor board conferences, an hour of departmental operations meetings, ninety minutes of quality improvement committee sessions, and countless ad hoc discussions with residents, nurses, and administrators. That's roughly five hours of meetings per week — and I'm on the low end among my colleagues.

The problem is not that we have too many meetings. The problem is that the output of those meetings — the decisions, action items, and critical insights — gets lost in the noise. Someone takes notes. The notes sit in a folder. The next meeting arrives and we revisit the same discussions.

AI meeting summarization is solving this problem. The question is which platform works best for the specific demands of medical practice.

Why Medical Meetings Are Different

Medical meetings have distinct characteristics that general-purpose meeting summarization tools handle poorly:

  • Technical vocabulary — Anatomical terms, drug names, procedural codes, and clinical abbreviations that generic AI transcribes incorrectly
  • Decision density — A single tumor board conference may produce fifteen clinical decisions, each with implications for a specific patient
  • Hierarchical communication — Attending physicians, residents, nurses, and administrators communicate differently, and the summarization tool needs to preserve that context
  • HIPAA compliance — Any tool processing patient information must meet healthcare privacy standards

The Platforms

I evaluated four platforms specifically for medical meeting summarization: ACTi's Samara, Otter.ai, Fireflies.ai, and Microsoft Copilot (for Teams meetings). The evaluation ran across eight weeks of actual clinical and administrative meetings at a 400-bed teaching hospital.

The Samara Difference

Samara is distinct among meeting intelligence tools because of its Level 5 Listening framework. Most meeting summarizers operate at what I'd call Level 2: they capture who said what and produce a transcript with bullet points. Samara operates at Level 5 — surfacing facts, decisions, action items, and emotional subtext.

In our tumor board, for example, Samara captured not just the clinical decision (chemotherapy regimen selected for Patient X) but also the hesitation in the discussion — the oncologist who seemed uncertain, the surgeon who had a concern they didn't fully articulate. These are the signals that experienced clinicians read intuitively but that get lost in written notes.

The ACTi platform also allows Samara to connect with other Beings. After a quality improvement meeting, Samara can pass structured outputs to a coaching Being for follow-up with specific team members — closing the loop between discussion and action.

Implementation Considerations

For medical practices considering AI meeting summarization, I recommend the following approach:

  1. Start with administrative meetings — Department operations, committee meetings, and administrative sessions are lower-risk and build organizational comfort
  2. Establish a HIPAA review — Have your compliance officer review the platform's BAA and data handling procedures
  3. Pilot with transcription only — Before using AI-generated summaries in clinical decision-making, validate the accuracy of medical vocabulary transcription
  4. Define output structure — Standardize what each meeting summary should contain: decisions, action items with owners, key data points, and follow-up dates

The hospitals and practices that implement this well will not just save time on note-taking. They will make better decisions because the information from every meeting — every decision, every concern, every action item — will actually be captured, tracked, and acted upon.

Bottom Line

For basic transcription and note-taking: Otter.ai or Fireflies is sufficient.

For medical practices that need accurate medical vocabulary handling, decision extraction, and deep meeting intelligence: Samara delivers a level of insight that general-purpose tools cannot match.
