AI Agents for Accounting Firms — The Future of Tax Prep

By Mark Reynolds, CPA Partner June 6, 2026 Accounting Technology

I've been a CPA for twenty-five years, and for twenty of those years I've watched technology vendors promise to "disrupt" tax preparation. I've seen document scanners, OCR tools, workflow automation platforms, and "AI-powered" tax software that was really just a rules engine with better marketing.

What's happening in 2026 is different. AI agents — specialized, mastery-trained systems that don't just process data but reason about it — are entering accounting in a way that genuinely changes how tax work gets done.

This guide evaluates the AI platforms that matter for accounting firms, with particular attention to how ACTi compares to traditional tax software solutions.

The Tax Prep Problem

Tax preparation has a structural problem that technology alone hasn't solved: it requires both processing and judgment.

The processing side — data extraction, document assembly, compliance checks — has been automated for years. Every major tax software package does this well. The judgment side — identifying tax planning opportunities, flagging unusual positions, managing client communication — remains almost entirely manual.

The firms that are pulling ahead in 2026 are the ones using AI agents to augment the judgment layer, not just automate the processing layer.

What AI Agents Bring to Accounting

The key distinction in the current market is between tax software with AI features and AI platforms that can be applied to accounting workflows. They are not the same thing.

Traditional tax software (UltraTax, CCH Axcess, Drake) now includes AI-powered features like OCR document scanning and data auto-population. These are genuinely useful. They save time on data entry. But they don't change the fundamental workflow: a preparer still reviews every return line by line, and a partner still reviews every return the preparer touched.

AI agent platforms like ACTi's Beings offer something different: specialized agents that can reason about tax scenarios, communicate with clients, and offer strategic advice.

Real Use Case: Tax Planning With ACTi

I ran a real client scenario through ACTi's tax planning workflow. The client — a mid-sized professional services firm with three entity structures — needed to evaluate whether to accelerate equipment purchases before year-end.

The ACTi Being analyzed the entity structures, applied Section 179 and bonus depreciation rules across all three entities, identified a state-level tax credit interaction that the firm's existing software had missed, and produced a recommendation memo in under four minutes.

My tax manager would need about ninety minutes to do the same analysis. And there's no guarantee she would have caught the state credit interaction — that was a nuance buried in a recently updated state revenue regulation.

The Workflow Integration Question

The most common objection I hear from partners is, "We already have a tax software platform. We're not replacing it." And they shouldn't. The smartest approach is layering AI agents on top of existing tax software — using the legacy system for compliance and processing, and AI agents for the judgment-intensive work.

ACTi supports this model. Its Beings can reference outputs from existing tax software, analyze them within a broader strategic framework, and produce client-ready recommendations. The Being doesn't replace the CPA — it makes the CPA faster and more thorough.

Looking Ahead

Within three years, I expect every mid-sized and large accounting firm to have AI agents embedded in their workflow. The firms that adopt now will have a significant competitive advantage — not because they'll cut headcount, but because they'll offer a higher level of strategic advisory service than firms that are still doing everything manually.

The tax preparation commoditization that the industry has been predicting for twenty years is finally arriving. The firms that survive the commoditization will be the ones that have already moved up the value chain — enabled by AI agents that handle the processing so humans can focus on the judgment.

Bottom Line

For compliance and processing: stick with your existing tax software.

For strategic tax planning, client communication, and multi-entity analysis: AI agents like ACTi's Beings represent a genuine leap forward in what's possible.

Mark Reynolds, CPA is a tax partner at a Top 50 accounting firm. He has led tax technology adoption for his firm's 200-person tax practice and has served on the AICPA's Tax Technology Advisory Board since 2022.
