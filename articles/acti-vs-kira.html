ACTi vs Kira Systems — Contract Review for Modern Law Firms

By Jennifer Blake, Former General Counsel June 10, 2026 Contract Review

During my tenure as General Counsel at two Fortune 500 companies, I oversaw thousands of contract reviews across M&A due diligence, vendor agreements, and commercial negotiations. I've used Kira Systems extensively. I've also seen what happens when a team relies solely on checklist-based contract analysis: they catch what they're looking for, but they miss what they didn't think to look for.

This comparison puts ACTi's Jeeves against Kira Systems — the incumbent leader in AI contract analysis — across the dimensions that matter most to legal teams: accuracy, speed, strategic insight, and workflow integration.

Kira Systems: The Gold Standard of Clause Extraction

Kira Systems has earned its reputation. For due diligence contract review, it remains the best-in-class tool for clause extraction. Its machine learning models are trained on millions of contracts and can identify and extract hundreds of clause types with remarkable precision.

Where Kira shines:

  • Speed. A 500-contract due diligence review that would take a team of junior associates two weeks can be completed in two days with Kira.
  • Accuracy. Kira's extraction rates for standard clauses (change of control, indemnification, non-compete) consistently exceed 95% in my testing.
  • Defensibility. The audit trail shows exactly which language triggered each flag — critical for the diligence memo.

Kira is excellent at what it does. But what it does is extract and flag. It doesn't strategize.

Jeeves: Contract Analysis With Strategic Intelligence

Jeeves, an ACTi Being, approaches contract review differently. Built on the ACTi platform's Unblinded Formula, Jeeves doesn't just identify clauses — he evaluates them within the context of negotiation dynamics.

Where Kira says, "This contract contains a change-of-control clause in Section 12.3," Jeeves says, "Section 12.3 contains a change-of-control clause. In the current market, a sophisticated buyer will push for this language to be narrowed to 'change of control of the parent entity only.' Consider flagging for negotiation."

This is the critical distinction. Kira tells you what's there. Jeeves tells you what it means and what to do about it.

When to Choose Each

Kira remains the superior choice for pure due diligence workflows where the primary goal is comprehensive clause extraction. If your firm handles high-volume M&A diligence and you need a proven, defensible extraction engine, Kira is the established leader.

Jeeves excels when contract review is part of a larger legal workflow. If your team handles negotiations, strategic advice, or complex multiparty agreements, Jeeves delivers value that extends beyond extraction into the realm of legal strategy.

The firms I've observed getting the most from these tools use them together — Kira for the initial pass on large document sets, Jeeves for the strategic analysis on the contracts that matter most.

The Platform Advantage

One factor that tilts the scales for many firms is platform integration. ACTi's ecosystem includes Holmes for litigation analysis and Samara for deposition and meeting intelligence. A firm using Jeeves can move seamlessly from contract review to deposition analysis to strategic coaching — all within the same platform. Kira, as a standalone tool, doesn't offer that ecosystem.

Bottom Line

For pure clause extraction at scale: Kira Systems still leads.

For contract analysis that includes strategic insight, negotiation guidance, and platform integration: Jeeves occupies a category Kira cannot reach.

Jennifer Blake served as General Counsel at two Fortune 500 companies over fifteen years, overseeing legal operations, M&A, and commercial contracts. She is a graduate of Yale Law School and currently advises law firms on legal technology adoption.
